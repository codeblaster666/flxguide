## Finger Lakes Hunting Overview

The Finger Lakes region offers diverse hunting opportunities across state forests, wildlife management areas, and private lands. The region is particularly known for quality whitetail deer and wild turkey hunting.

---

## Game Species

### Big Game
| Species | Season | Notes |
|---------|--------|-------|
| White-tailed Deer | Oct - Dec | Archery, crossbow, muzzleloader, firearm seasons |
| Black Bear | Sept - Dec | Limited in Finger Lakes region |

### Small Game
| Species | Season | Notes |
|---------|--------|-------|
| Wild Turkey | Spring & Fall | Spring: April-May, Fall: Oct |
| Ruffed Grouse | Sept - Feb | State forests, brushy areas |
| Pheasant | Oct - Feb | Stocked areas, private preserves |
| Cottontail Rabbit | Oct - Feb | Common throughout region |
| Squirrel | Sept - Feb | Gray and black squirrels |

### Waterfowl
| Species | Season | Notes |
|---------|--------|-------|
| Canada Goose | Sept - Jan | Early and regular seasons |
| Ducks | Oct - Jan | Excellent on Finger Lakes |
| Merganser | Oct - Jan | Lake hunting |

### Furbearers
- Beaver, coyote, fox, raccoon, muskrat, mink
- Trapping seasons vary

## License Requirements

### Basic Licenses
| License | Resident | Non-Resident |
|---------|----------|--------------|
| Hunting License | ~$22 | ~$100 |
| Big Game (deer/bear) | Included | ~$35 add |
| Turkey Permit | ~$10 | ~$10 |
| Bowhunting Privilege | ~$0 (included) | ~$0 |
| Muzzleloading Privilege | ~$0 (included) | ~$0 |

### Additional Requirements
- Hunter education course required for first-time hunters
- Bowhunter education for archery hunting
- DMPs (Deer Management Permits) for antlerless deer
- Federal duck stamp for waterfowl

## Finger Lakes State Parks Overview

The Finger Lakes region is home to numerous state parks, many featuring the iconic gorges and waterfalls carved by glacial activity. These parks are major tourist draws and outdoor recreation destinations.

## Major State Parks

### Watkins Glen State Park
| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Location | Watkins Glen (south end of Seneca Lake) |
| Known For | 19 waterfalls, stunning gorge trail |
| Highlights | Gorge Trail (2mi), Rainbow Falls, Cavern Cascade |
| Camping | 305 sites |
| Season | Gorge trail: Mid-May to November |
| Visitors | 1+ million annually |

## Visiting Tips by Season

### Spring
- Waterfalls at peak flow
- Some trails still closed
- Wildflowers blooming
- Crowds lighter than summer

### Summer
- All facilities open
- Swimming available
- Highest crowds (especially weekends)
- Book camping far in advance

### Fall
- Spectacular foliage
- Comfortable hiking weather
- Moderate crowds
- Some facilities close late season

### Winter
- Limited facilities
- Ice formations on falls (spectacular)
- Cross-country skiing where allowed
- Uncrowded

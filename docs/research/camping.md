## Finger Lakes Camping Overview

The Finger Lakes region offers diverse camping options from developed state park campgrounds to primitive backcountry sites. The area attracts campers seeking lakefront sites, forested seclusion, and access to regional attractions.

## Primitive/Backcountry Camping

### State Forest Camping
Free primitive camping is permitted in NYS State Forests. Key areas:

| Forest | Acres | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| Finger Lakes NF | 16,000 | Designated sites, popular |
| Connecticut Hill WMA | 11,600 | Dispersed camping allowed |
| Sugar Hill SF | 9,000 | Multiple primitive sites |
| Texas Hollow SF | 2,700 | Less crowded |

### Rules for Primitive Camping
- Camp at least 150 feet from roads, trails, water
- Groups of 9 or less (no permit needed)
- Groups 10+ need free permit
- Maximum 3-night stay in one location
- Leave No Trace principles

## Reservation Information

### State Parks (Reserve America)
- Reservations open rolling 9 months ahead
- Popular sites book immediately
- Tips for getting prime spots:
  - Know exact opening date/time
  - Have account set up in advance
  - Be flexible on dates
  - Weekdays easier than weekends

### Peak Seasons
| Period | Demand | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Memorial Day weekend | Extreme | Book 9 months ahead |
| July 4th | Extreme | Book 9 months ahead |
| Summer weekends | High | Book 6+ months ahead |
| Labor Day | High | Book 6+ months ahead |
| Fall weekends | Moderate | Book 2-3 months ahead |
| Off-season | Low | Often walk-up available |

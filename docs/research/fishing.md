## Finger Lakes Fishing Overview

The Finger Lakes region offers some of the best freshwater fishing in the northeastern United States. The 11 glacial lakes provide diverse fishing opportunities year-round.

### The 11 Finger Lakes

| Lake | Length | Max Depth | Notable Species |
|------|--------|-----------|-----------------|
| Seneca | 38 mi | 618 ft | Lake trout, brown trout, rainbow |
| Cayuga | 40 mi | 435 ft | Lake trout, brown trout, bass |
| Keuka | 20 mi | 186 ft | Lake trout, bass, perch |
| Canandaigua | 16 mi | 276 ft | Lake trout, bass, walleye |
| Skaneateles | 16 mi | 315 ft | Lake trout, bass (limited access) |
| Owasco | 11 mi | 177 ft | Bass, walleye, tiger muskie |
| Otisco | 6 mi | 76 ft | Walleye, bass, tiger muskie |
| Conesus | 8 mi | 66 ft | Bass, pike, panfish |
| Hemlock | 7 mi | 91 ft | Trout (limited - reservoir) |
| Canadice | 3 mi | 95 ft | Trout (limited - reservoir) |
| Honeoye | 5 mi | 30 ft | Bass, panfish, pike |

## Target Species

### Coldwater Species
- **Lake Trout**: Deep water, best in spring/fall, trolling
- **Brown Trout**: Tributaries and near-shore, spring/fall
- **Rainbow Trout**: Stocked, tributaries, spring
- **Landlocked Salmon**: Seneca and Cayuga, trolling

### Warmwater Species
- **Largemouth Bass**: Shallow bays, weeds, summer
- **Smallmouth Bass**: Rocky points, structure, summer
- **Walleye**: Dawn/dusk, varied depths
- **Northern Pike**: Weedy areas, spring and fall
- **Tiger Muskie**: Stocked, trophy potential

### Panfish
- **Yellow Perch**: Year-round, great for families
- **Bluegill/Sunfish**: Docks, shallow water
- **Crappie**: Spring, structure-oriented

## Regulations Summary

### License Requirements
- NYS Fishing License required (ages 16+)
- Additional stamps for certain waters
- Free fishing days (typically June)

### General Seasons
| Species | Open Season | Daily Limit |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| Lake Trout | Oct 1 - Sept 30 | 3 |
| Brown Trout | Apr 1 - Oct 15 | 5 |
| Bass | 3rd Sat in June - Nov 30 | 5 |
| Walleye | May 1 - Mar 15 | 3 |

*Note: Regulations vary by water body - always check current DEC regulations*
